Apple Ledger User Guide

User Guide

Set up ledgers and accounts, record transactions, review budgets and reports, track recurring bills, and protect local finance data.

Apple Ledger Support Privacy Policy
1

Set Up Your Ledger

  1. Create a ledger for a person, family, project, or business scenario.
  2. Add accounts such as cash, bank cards, credit cards, deposits, loans, or other assets and liabilities.
  3. Organize categories, tags, merchants, projects, and members before recording daily transactions.
2

Record and Review Transactions

  1. Add expenses, income, transfers, refunds, reimbursements, loans, installments, fees, or balance adjustments.
  2. Attach notes and receipt images when they help explain a record.
  3. Use dashboard cards, category rankings, merchant rankings, and trend charts to review spending and net worth.
3

Budgets, Recurring Bills, and Reminders

  1. Create budgets by month or category, then review progress and threshold reminders.
  2. Use recurring templates and subscription items to track bills such as rent, services, memberships, and subscriptions you pay elsewhere.
  3. Grant notification permission if you want local reminders for subscriptions, recurring entries, budgets, deposits, credit cards, or installments.
4

Privacy, Sync, and Backup

  1. Finance data is stored locally. Optional iCloud sync uses your private iCloud account through CloudKit.
  2. Enable the privacy lock to protect the app with Face ID, Touch ID, or device authentication.
  3. Use JSON backup and restore for full app data, or CSV import/export for transaction workflows.
  4. Widget, Watch, App Shortcuts, and macOS menu bar entries use Apple platform sharing and should be tested with the final App Group and iCloud container before release.
